here is a summary of findings (written by gemini):

1. The "Heavy Hitters" (Positive Influence)
The features at the top are what the model associates with 
higher popularity:
Explicit Content & Pop Genre:
These are your strongest positive predictors. 
Effectively, the model sees that being in the "Pop" genre or 
having "Explicit" lyrics gives a song a slight popularity 
boost compared to the rest of the filtered dataset.

Energy: This has a tiny positive effect. 
It suggests that, all else being equal, slightly more energetic
songs perform better, but the impact is very small 
($0.0018$).

2. The "Popularity Killers" (Negative Influence)
The features at the bottom are what the model associates 
with lower popularity:
Synth-Pop & Indie-Pop: 
Interestingly, in this specific filtered group, being labeled 
as "Synth-Pop" or "Indie-Pop" actually correlates with lower 
popularity scores.
Duration & Acousticness: Longer songs and 
more acoustic songs tend to be less popular in this dataset.

3. The "Noise" (Near-Zero Influence)
Look at features like instrumentalness, liveness, and key. 
Their coefficients are incredibly close to zero 
(e.g., $0.000078$).
Plain English: These features basically do not matter to the 
model. Changing the key of a song or making it slightly 
more "live" has almost zero predictable impact on whether 
the song is popular or not.

The "Big Picture" Reality Check
While we can see which features "win," there is a catch. 
Most of these coefficients are very small (below $0.1$).
In a linear regression, if the coefficients are this tiny 
and your $R^2$ is low (which we saw earlier), it tells us 
that musical features are not a good "crystal ball" for 
success.

The Verdict: Your model is saying: 
"If I absolutely had to guess, I'd say a short, 
non-acoustic Pop song with explicit lyrics will be 
popular—but honestly, I'm mostly guessing because 
these features don't explain the whole story.
"This is a great finding for your team because it proves 
that human elements (like who the artist is or how much 
the song is played on TikTok) likely matter way more than 
the technical "audio" settings.
